Tyler1 played 3.8k top games (over 2 seasons + preseason), failing master promos 35+ times before hitting challenger on top. In one session, he played 25 games straight, losing 18 of them. Another time, he played 30 games in a single session - so many that it didn't even fit within a 24-hour period. That is bordering on inhuman levels of psychological torture especially if you tilt as badly he does. Although top was clearly the most hellish he also grinded to challenger in every other role. And that's to say nothing of chess, where he peaked ~1960 on chess.com starting from basically zero experience with a similar "brute force" strategy. Until recently I had a very positive opinion; "you have to respect the grind if nothing else," but I read more about it and learned he's acknowledged having an extremely addictive personality. Tyler certainly has tolerance for the "grind" but it does seem different when it's more a byproduct of serious addiction. I suppose commitment to that degree nearly by definition has to be an addiction of some sort. In any case, that does not seem replicable (or necessarily desirable) for us mortals.
